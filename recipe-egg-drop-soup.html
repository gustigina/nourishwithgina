Egg Drop Soup

⏱ 15 min
👤 4 servings
🔥 ~120 kcal
💪 45g protein

Ingredients

Broth
  • 1.2 litres water
  • 2 tsp chicken stock powder (or homemade chicken broth)
  • 4–5 thin slices fresh ginger
  • 3–4 garlic cloves, crushed
  • ¾–1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp white pepper
Thickener
  • 2 tbsp cornstarch (maizena)
  • 4 tbsp cold water, mix with cornstarch first before adding to pot
Eggs & Finishing
  • 6 eggs, beaten until smooth
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • Spring onions (daun bawang), thinly sliced
  • Extra white pepper to taste

How to Make It

  1. 01

    Build the broth. Add water, stock powder, ginger slices, and crushed garlic to a pot. Bring to a boil. Reduce heat and let simmer 3–4 minutes so the aromatics infuse. Remove ginger and garlic if you prefer a cleaner texture.

  2. 02

    Mix your cornstarch slurry. Combine 2 tbsp cornstarch with 4 tbsp cold water in a small bowl. Stir until fully dissolved, no lumps. This step must be done before it goes into the hot pot.

  3. 03

    Thicken the broth. Reduce heat to medium. Pour in the cornstarch slurry while stirring constantly. The broth will thicken and turn silky within 30 seconds. This glossy texture is what makes the egg ribbons so beautiful.

  4. 04

    The egg ribbons, the key step. Reduce heat to the lowest possible setting. Beat 6 eggs until smooth. Because the volume is large, pour in two batches: slowly drizzle half the egg while stirring the broth in slow circular motions. Wait 15 seconds, then pour the second half. Keep stirring gently, never fast. The eggs will form long silky ribbons.

  5. 05

    Finish and serve. Turn off heat. Add sesame oil and extra white pepper. Ladle into bowls, top generously with sliced spring onions. Drink it warm.

🌿 Chef's Note from Gina

"The egg ribbons will tell you if you did it right. If they come out silky and long, you nailed the heat. If they're clumped or scrambled, the heat was too high or you poured too fast. Low heat, slow pour, gentle stir. That's the whole secret."
