Korean Beef Bowl

⏱ 30 min
👤 2 servings
🔥 655 kcal
💪 41g protein

Ingredients

For the beef
  • 250g lean ground beef (or thinly sliced sirloin)
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce (low sodium preferred)
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 1 tbsp honey or maple syrup
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 tsp cornstarch
For the bowl
  • 1.5 cups cooked brown rice (or cauliflower rice)
  • 2 cups mixed greens or spinach
  • 1 medium cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup shredded carrots
  • 2 soft-boiled eggs
  • Sesame seeds, to garnish
  • Spring onions, sliced
  • Kimchi, optional but encouraged

How to make it

  1. 01

    Marinate the beef. Combine soy sauce, sesame oil, gochujang, honey, garlic, ginger, and cornstarch in a bowl. Add beef and toss to coat. Let it rest for at least 15 minutes — this is where the magic happens. If you have time, 30 minutes gives you restaurant-level depth.

  2. 02

    Cook your rice. While the beef is marinating, cook your brown rice. I use a rice cooker but stovetop works fine. Season lightly with salt and a few drops of sesame oil once done.

  3. 03

    Sear the beef. Heat a pan or wok over high heat. When it's very hot (almost smoking), add the beef. Don't touch it for 90 seconds — let it caramelize. Then break it up and cook until the sauce is thickened and glossy. This should take 4–5 minutes total.

  4. 04

    Soft boil your eggs. Bring water to a boil, gently lower eggs in, cook exactly 7 minutes. Transfer to ice water immediately. Peel and halve when cooled.

  5. 05

    Assemble with intention. Start with a base of greens, then rice. Lay the beef on one side, arrange cucumber and carrots on the other. Place egg halves on top. Scatter sesame seeds and spring onions. Add kimchi on the side. Eat immediately — this is a bowl meant to be eaten hot and fast.

🌿 Chef's Note from Gina

"This bowl was born on a Tuesday when I was exhausted and craving something that felt indulgent but wasn't. The gochujang gives it that guilty-tasting kick — but it's actually full of probiotics. Brown rice keeps you full. Lean beef has all the iron your brain needs. Soft egg yolk? That's your serotonin precursor, tryptophan, right there. Eat it. Enjoy it. It loves you back."

🧠 Brain Food Note

Gochujang contains capsaicin, which triggers endorphin release. The egg yolk delivers choline — critical for acetylcholine, the neurotransmitter of memory and focus. Brown rice provides steady glucose, keeping your mood stable rather than spiking and crashing. This is a bowl that feeds your mind, not just your body.
