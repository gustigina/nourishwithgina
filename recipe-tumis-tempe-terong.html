Tumis Tempe & Terong Pedas Manis

⏱ 20 min
👤 2 servings
🔥 650 kcal
💪 45g protein

Ingredients

Protein & Vegetables
  • 200g tempeh (tempe), cut into ~2cm cubes
  • 2 medium eggplants (terong ungu), cut into ~2cm cubes
Sauce & Seasoning
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 3–4 shallots (bawang merah), thinly sliced
  • 1 red + 1 green bell pepper (paprika), sliced, or substitute with fresh chilies
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • ½ tsp honey (or palm sugar)
  • ½ tsp sesame oil
  • 4 tbsp water
  • Salt + black pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil (for stir-frying)
  • Korean chili powder, sprinkle at the end (optional)

How to Make It

  1. 01

    Prep the eggplant, do not skip. Cut eggplant into ~2cm cubes. Sprinkle lightly with salt, let sit 5 minutes, then pat completely dry with a paper towel. This removes bitterness and prevents the eggplant from absorbing too much oil.

  2. 02

    Fry the tempeh until golden. Heat pan with 1.5 tbsp olive oil over medium-high heat. Fry tempeh cubes until golden on all sides, about 4 minutes. Remove and set aside. Pre-frying keeps it firm and prevents it from falling apart later.

  3. 03

    Stir-fry the aromatics. In the same pan, add ½ tbsp olive oil. Sauté shallots for 1 minute until soft, then add garlic and bell peppers (or chilies). Stir-fry 1 minute until fragrant.

  4. 04

    Cook the eggplant. Add eggplant to the pan. Toss with the aromatics. Add 3–4 tbsp water, cover for 3 minutes. Uncover and stir until eggplant is soft but not falling apart.

  5. 05

    Add sauce and finish. Pour in soy sauce and honey. Stir, the sauce will caramelize and turn glossy. Add the fried tempeh back in. Add sesame oil, salt, and pepper. Toss everything together for 1 minute and remove from heat.

  6. 06

    Serve. Plate over Couscous Basic or rice. Sprinkle with Korean chili powder and sesame seeds if desired. Best eaten hot.

🌿 Chef's Note from Gina

"Fry the tempeh separately first, this is non-negotiable. Tempeh needs its own crispy crust before going into the sauce, otherwise it turns soft and disappears into everything. And do not skip the salt-and-pat step on the eggplant. Five minutes makes the difference between silky eggplant and a greasy pan."

🧠 Brain Food Note

Tempeh is one of the most bioavailable plant proteins, fermentation breaks down the phytic acid that normally blocks nutrient absorption. Combined with eggplant's anthocyanins (brain-protective antioxidants) and the anti-inflammatory properties of garlic and sesame, this is one of the most nutrient-dense stir-fries in Indonesian cuisine.
