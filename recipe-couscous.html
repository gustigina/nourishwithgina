Couscous Basic

⏱ 15 min
👤 2 servings
🔥 650 kcal
💪 45g protein

Ingredients

What you need
  • 1 cup dry couscous
  • 2 cups hot water (boiled)
  • Pinch of salt
  • Pinch of nutmeg
  • Pinch of smoked paprika
  • Pinch of turmeric
  • 1 tsp olive oil (optional)

How to Make It

  1. 01

    Mix the marinade. Combine soy sauce, mirin, sesame oil, lime juice, grated garlic, grated red onion, smoked paprika, and chili powder in a bowl. Stir until combined.

  2. 02

    Slice the beef. Cut into 3–4cm pieces. Against the grain if possible.

  3. 03

    Get your pan screaming hot. No oil. High heat. Wait until you can feel the heat rising from the pan surface.

  4. 04

    Sear the beef. Add beef in small batches. Do not touch for 45 seconds — let the edges char. Flip, 30 more seconds.

  5. 05

    Add the marinade. Pour the marinade over the beef, toss everything for 1–1.5 minutes until the sauce is glossy and coats every piece.

  6. 06

    Remove immediately. Do not overcook. The residual heat continues to cook it after it leaves the pan.

  7. 07

    Build your bowl. Serve over couscous or rice. Add Air Fryer Cherry Tomato, Mayak Eggs, and romaine lettuce for a full Korean Beef Bowl.

🌿 Chef's Note from Gina

"The spices go in dry — before the water. This way they bloom into the grains as they absorb. Just a pinch of each is enough: you want warmth in the background, not a spice bomb. Cover tightly and do not peek. Bonus tip: if you have cooking liquid from rice cooker chicken, mix it in instead of plain water — the flavor it adds is extraordinary."

🧠 Brain Food Note

Turmeric contains curcumin — one of the most studied neuroprotective compounds in food. Even a small pinch activates anti-inflammatory pathways in the brain. Paired with paprika (vitamin C, carotenoids) and nutmeg (myristicin, linked to cognitive support), this is a deceptively powerful base.
